	Casino Gaming Glossary for Beginners
	Navigating the world of online casinos is more enjoyable when you understand the language. This glossary explains the key terms you'll encounter at K777W, helping you play with greater confidence. We've defined everything in simple terms to get you started.
	Bankroll	The total amount of money a player sets aside specifically for gambling. Managing your bankroll responsibly is the key to a healthy and enjoyable gaming experience.
	Bonus	A promotional offer given by the casino to players. Common bonuses include welcome offers for new players, free spins on slot games, or extra cash added to your deposit.
	House Edge	The built-in mathematical advantage the casino has in any game. It's a small percentage of bets that the casino expects to win over the long term, ensuring its profitability.
	Jackpot	The largest possible prize a player can win from a single game, most commonly found in slot machines. Some jackpots are progressive, meaning the prize amount grows with each bet placed until it is won.
	Live Dealer	A feature in online casinos where you can play table games with a real human dealer via a live video stream. This creates an immersive and authentic casino atmosphere from your own home.
	Payline	In a slot game, a payline is a specific line on the reels where matching symbols must land to create a winning combination. Modern video slots can have dozens or even hundreds of paylines.
	RNG (Random Number Generator)	A sophisticated computer algorithm that ensures the outcome of every spin, card deal, or dice roll is completely random and unpredictable. This technology guarantees fairness and integrity in all online casino games.
	RTP (Return to Player)	The theoretical percentage of all wagered money that a specific game will pay back to players over a very long period of time. A higher RTP indicates a lower house edge and, theoretically, better odds for the player.
	Stake	The amount of money a player wagers, or bets, on a single spin, hand, or round of a game. This is also referred to as a 'wager'.
	Wagering Requirement	A condition applied to casino bonuses that specifies the number of times a player must bet the bonus amount before any associated winnings can be withdrawn as real money.
